The kit

What I shoot with

Honest answer: the camera matters less than the eye behind it. Here is the small, deliberate kit I actually carry. Real photos of each body are on the way.

01 / Primary

Sony a6300

My workhorse. Fast, light, and quiet enough to disappear on the street. The APS-C sensor and quick autofocus make it the body I reach for on most editorial and portrait work.

  • Best for: street, editorial, portrait, travel
  • Why it stays: small footprint, dependable focus

02 / The all-rounder

Canon Rebel T7

The dependable DSLR. A warm, forgiving look straight out of camera and a deep lens ecosystem. It is where a lot of people start, and it still earns its place in the bag for setups and second-body days.

  • Best for: portraits, landscapes, set work
  • Why it stays: reliable color, classic optical feel

03 / Below the surface

Nikon 1 + underwater housing

The wildcard. Sealed in its underwater shell, the Nikon 1 opens up a whole world the other bodies cannot reach: pools, surf, rain, rivers, and the strange light you only find underwater.

  • Best for: underwater, water sports, wet weather
  • Why it stays: goes where no other body can
